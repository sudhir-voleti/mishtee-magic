MishTee-Magic (Part B): The Delivery Mitra Integration

Firm Context

In Lec03, we successfully built a core DB-in-cloud solution for MishTee-Magic's data woes.

We further D-B-Ded the cust view of the MishTee app - the simplest of the 3 views we designed.

Time now to pivot to the deliv-agent web-app view wherein we wrestle with the logistical challenge of Ahmedabad's notoriously unpredictable traffic.

Q is "How do we ensure a "MishTee" treat arrives before it loses its crunch?"

MishTee has partnered with Delivery Mitra for hyperlocal logistics. Their agents need a dedicated view within our app to manage their routes.

Ask yourself, what minimal UI features would you expect in a deliv agent's app-view? Design always starts thus, only.

Turns out there's a third-party Hyperlocal Traffic API we'd have to integrate our app with to solve the traffic riddle, and obtain real-time congestion signals across the city. Forward, Ahoy!

Internal Memo

To: Product Lead (You)

From: Anjali, CEO

Subject: Delivery-Agent View Requirements

We need to empower our Delivery Mitra partners with a clear, efficient interface. I am looking for a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) for the agent view.

The interface must show:

  • Secure agent login with their phone numbers
  • Orders assigned to the agent
  • Precise store locations for pickups
  • Customer addresses for delivery location
  • Cust phone numbers for coordination if needed
  • A Google Maps widget displaying the pickup and drop-off locations
  • Show traffic congestion status
  • Estimated delivery times (ETD)
  • Ability to update order status from 'pending' to 'delivered'

Well, yeah, that's quite a bit, I know. Still, we don't have the luxury of much time.

Can you pilot a quick prototype for this? Something we can later productize and deploy at scale.

Appendix: Third-Party Traffic API Documentation (v1.2)

We use the HyperLocal-Traffic service to pull real-time data. We now pivot to the technical specifications required for our integration.

Endpoint Method Required Inputs Principal Outputs
/get-congestion GET lat, lon congestion_score (1-10)
/get-etd GET origin_lat/lon, dest_lat/lon etd_minutes (Int)

What would you like to see happen if the etd_minutes exceeds 60 minutes for a 5km trip?
